# Core Principles
> 🟢 Foundational concepts behind all privacy practices.

## Data Minimization
Don't share what you don't have to. Use aliases, deny unnecessary permissions, delete unused accounts.

## Compartmentalization
Keep identities separate. Use different browsers, emails, and devices per context.

## Zero Trust
Assume any service or network can be compromised. Prefer E2EE over trusting operators.

## Open Source Preference
Prefer audited open-source tools over closed-source "trust us" claims.

## Defense in Depth
Layer your protections:
1. Network (VPN, DNS, firewall)
2. Application (browser, email, messaging)
3. OS (encryption, sandboxing, updates)
4. Behavior (habits, OPSEC)

## The "Nothing to Hide" Fallacy
Privacy is about control over your own information. Data collected today can be weaponized under future laws.
